Department of Anthropology Library


Anthropology is a fascinating field that explores the complexities of human societies and cultures. It draws on a wide range of disciplines, including biology, history, sociology, linguistics, and archaeology, to understand the diverse ways in which people live and interact with their environment. The Department of Anthropology library is an essential resource for scholars and students interested in this field, providing access to a vast collection of books, journals, and other materials that cover a broad range of topics.

The Department of Anthropology library is located on the main campus of University of Yangon, and it has a long and rich history. The library was established in 2018 with volunteering students, and over the years, it has grown to become one of the largest and most comprehensive collections of anthropological literature in the world. The library's collections cover all areas of anthropology, including cultural anthropology, archaeology, biological anthropology, and linguistic anthropology.

One of the most impressive features of the Department of Anthropology library is its vast collection of books. The library has over 100,000 volumes, including rare and out-of-print works, as well as contemporary publications. The library's books cover a wide range of topics, including ethnography, archaeology, human evolution, linguistic anthropology, and cultural studies. Whether you are a student or a researcher, the Department of Anthropology library has something for everyone.

In addition to its extensive book collection, the Department of Anthropology library also provides access to a wide range of journals and other periodicals. The library subscribes to over 300 anthropology-related journals, including some of the most prestigious and influential publications in the field. The library's journal collection covers a wide range of topics, from the latest research in human evolution to contemporary issues in cultural anthropology.

The Department of Anthropology library also offers a range of other resources to support its users. The library has an extensive collection of audiovisual materials, including films, documentaries, and other media that can help to bring the study of anthropology to life. The library also has a number of special collections, including rare books and manuscripts, archival materials, and other unique resources that can provide valuable insights into the history and development of anthropology as a discipline.

Finally, the Department of Anthropology library provides a range of services to support its users. The library's staff are highly knowledgeable and experienced in the field of anthropology, and they are always available to provide assistance and advice to users. The library also offers a range of training and instruction sessions, including workshops on research methods, citation management, and other topics that can help users to get the most out of the library's resources.

In conclusion, the Department of Anthropology library is an invaluable resource for scholars and students of anthropology. With its vast collection of books, journals, and other materials, as well as its knowledgeable staff and range of services, the library provides a rich and stimulating environment for research and learning. Whether you are a seasoned researcher or a new student of anthropology, the Department of Anthropology library is a place where you can explore the fascinating world of human societies and cultures.

Department of Anthropology Museum


Anthropology museums often have extensive collections of objects from different cultures, ranging from traditional clothing and tools to religious artifacts and art. These collections can help visitors to understand the beliefs, practices, and traditions of different cultures, as well as the ways in which these cultures have interacted with each other over time. Many anthropology museums also feature exhibits that explore the social and political contexts in which these artifacts were created and used.

In addition to their collections, anthropology museums often offer a range of educational programs and events. These programs may include lectures, workshops, and demonstrations on topics related to anthropology, such as archaeology, cultural anthropology, and linguistics. Many museums also offer guided tours, which can provide visitors with a more in-depth understanding of the collections and exhibits.

Anthropology museums are not just repositories of artifacts and knowledge - they are also important cultural institutions that reflect the values and perspectives of the societies in which they are located. Many anthropology museums strive to promote social justice and cultural awareness by challenging visitors to confront their own assumptions and biases, and by presenting a more inclusive and diverse view of human societies and cultures.

Finally, anthropology museums also play an important role in preserving and protecting cultural heritage. Many of the artifacts and specimens in these museums are rare or unique, and may be in danger of being lost or destroyed due to political conflicts, environmental degradation, or other factors. By preserving and displaying these materials, anthropology museums can help to ensure that future generations will have access to a rich and diverse cultural heritage.

In conclusion, anthropology museums are fascinating and important cultural institutions that provide visitors with a unique window into the diversity and complexity of human societies and cultures. With their collections of artifacts and specimens, educational programs, and commitment to social justice and cultural awareness, anthropology museums play a vital role in preserving and celebrating our shared human heritage.
