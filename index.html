What is Anthropology?


Anthropology is the study of humans, past and present, in all their aspects, including biological, social, cultural, and linguistic. Anthropologists seek to understand the diversity of human experience, behavior, and organization, from the earliest human ancestors to contemporary societies around the world. Anthropology involves both the study of the biological evolution of humans and the analysis of cultural and social practices and beliefs. It is a broad and interdisciplinary field that draws on many other disciplines, including archaeology, sociology, psychology, linguistics, and biology, among others. Anthropologists conduct research in diverse settings, including academic institutions, museums, government agencies, and communities around the world. Their work includes fieldwork, laboratory research, and the analysis of historical and contemporary data. The ultimate goal of anthropology is to contribute to a greater understanding of human diversity and to use that understanding to promote social and cultural understanding and tolerance.
